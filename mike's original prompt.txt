Prompt:  Create a web app that builds a ladder of TIPS (treasury inflation protected securities) to provide a constant after-tax real dollar (not nominal dollar) cash flow in each calendar year of the ladder.  Cash flow for a calendar year should consist of (i) (a) coupon payments on the TIPS owned by the user of the app maturing in such calendar year or in later calendar years, plus (b) the principal of TIPS owned by the user maturing in such calendar year, minus (ii) the marginal income tax rate specified by the user multiplied by (a) the coupon payments on any TIPS owned by the user that are held in any account that is not a Roth account and (b) the principal of any TIPS owned by the user held in a pretax account (such as a pretax IRA or 401(k)) that mature in such calendar  year.  The user should be able to specify (1) the user’s marginal income tax rate, (2) the calendar years to be covered by the ladder, (3) the desired real dollar income desired to be produced by the TIPS ladder, specified as a dollar amount as of a calendar year specified by the user of the app, (4) whether there are any calendar years that should have a different real income and the amount of real income desired in each such specified calendar year, and (5) (a) the number of TIPS (each TIPS having an original principal about of $1,000 nominal dollars as of its issuance) already owned by the user in each ladder year, (b) the CUSIP number of such TIPS or the coupon rate and maturity date of such TIPS, and the type or types of accounts (Roth, pretax, or taxable brokerage) in which each such TIPS is held.  The output of the app should be a list of the years included in the ladder, with a designation for each such year of the amount by which the user’s already-owned TIPS falls short of or exceeds the desired after-tax real income. 

Note to self:  Later versions of the app should (1) automatically inflate the specified desired after-tax real income from the originally specified year to the then current year, (2) for over-funded years, calculate and specify how many TIPS may be sold in the various types of accounts (which may be adjusted by the user) to come closer to the desired real income for the various over-funded years, (3) for under-funded years, calculate and specify how many TIPS need to be acquired in the various types of accounts (which may be adjusted by the user) to come closer to the desired real income for the various under-funded years, and (4) allow the user to specify an expected inflation rate so that the cash flow in each calendar year can be reduced by the specified marginal income tax rate multiplied by the amount by which the principal amount of TIPS held in a taxable brokerage account will increase in each calendar year (based on such expected inflation rate).
